ABOUT COMPUTER

ABOUT COMPUTER A computer is an electronic device that processes raw data into actionable
information by following instructions, operating through a cycle of input,
processing, storage, and output. It consists of hardware (physical parts like CPU,
RAM) and software (programs) to perform tasks, ranging from basic calculations to
internet browsing and multimedia creation.

Key Aspects of Computers:

  • Core Functions: All computers, from smartphones to supercomputers, accept input, process it via a Central Processing Unit (CPU), store data, and produce output.
  • Hardware Components: Essential parts include the motherboard, processor (CPU), memory (RAM), storage (hard drive/SSD), and power supply.
  • Software: Includes the Operating System (e.g., Windows, macOS, Linux) that manages hardware, and application software used for specific tasks like web browsing or editing documents.
  • Types: Computers come in various forms, including desktop computers, laptops, tablets, smartphones, and servers.
  • History: Early computing began with mechanical devices like the Antikythera mechanism (c. 100 BCE), but modern computing evolved through electronic devices such as ENIAC in the 1940s and the development of the microprocessor in the 1970s.
  • Uses: Computers are used for, but not limited to, communication (email, video calls), entertainment (gaming, streaming), education, business (data analysis, spreadsheets), and scientific research.
